Do I have any weirdly specific opinions on package delivery companies?

Why yes, I do, I'm glad you asked! Observe:

  1. USPS: The gold standard. They might always have financial issues (because of Congress), but they get the job done well and on time, and they bring postal service to some truly ludicrous places. Plus, they're the only people legally allowed to deliver to mailboxes. That's pretty neat.
  2. UPS: Losing that single "S" from their name takes them down a notch, but there's potential here. Your packages might go through the ringer, but they keep a good schedule. If they say it'll be there Friday, it'll be there Friday. They also treat their drivers well and promote up from within the company, so that's nice.
  3. Amazon: True, a lot of what comes from Amazon is delivered by other companies, but they handle a good amount themselves too. They're usually pretty solid, but if your package gets delayed once it is likely to be delayed again. Don't always trust their delivery estimates. Line worker conditions are much better than UPS though, but drivers are a different story. It's not nearly as sweet a gig for them.
  4. DHS Logistics: A bit of an up and comer, in my opinion, though I honestly have no idea how big they are. I haven't interacted with them much, but they seem broadly competent.
  5. Hand Delivery: Delivering a package yourself may be tedious, but it's better than the next option.
  6. Fedex: I have a burning hatred for this company. Every interaction I've had with them has been bad, or at best, worse than any of their competitors. Their delivery estimates are a joke, and will fluctuate like crazy without any meaning. Their hiring process is slower than molasses. They also have a bad relationship with their drivers. They do one cool thing with their logo and then coast on their reputation. Avoid this company if at all possible.

Anyway, those are my opinions on that.
